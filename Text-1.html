春江花月夜

About


Background

The music Moonlight of the Spring River is based on a Tang poem with the same name. Written by Zhang Ruoxu (張若虛), the poem was highly appreciated and was appraised as a poem that left him for fame in Tang Dynasty (孤篇橫絕全唐). The music was based on a piece of pipa music called Xiao and Drums in the sunset (夕陽蕭鼓) created in about 18th century. Ensemble versions were created in about 1930s. The music was being rewritten into different versions. Now Moonlight of the Spring River is one of the ten most famous traditional Chinese music.

Structure


The whole music in general can be divided into ten parts. Each part of the music describes a scenery. One thing worth mentioning is that the ending of each part is the same. This is designed to bring back the audience back to the great picture of Moonlight of the Spring River. The ten parts, or the ten sceneries, are:

1. Xiao and Drums in the Sunset (夕陽蕭鼓)
2. Flowers Blowing Back by Wind 花蕊散回風)
3. Where the Mountain Meets the Moon (關山臨卻月)
4. Sunset by the Water (臨水斜陽)
5. Sound of Reed and Maple in the Autumn (楓荻秋聲)
6. Scenery of Wu Gorge (巫峽千尋)
7. Music of Xiao in the Woods (簫聲紅樹里)
8. Nightview of the River (臨江晚眺)
9. Songs from the Fishing Boats (漁舟唱晚)
10. Returning Fishing Boat in Sunset (夕陽影裡一歸舟)
